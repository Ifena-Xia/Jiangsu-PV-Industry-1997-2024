# Jiangsu's Photovoltaic Industry (1997–2024)

**Live site:** https://ifena-xia.github.io/Jiangsu-PV-Industry-1997-2024/

A process-tracing study of how Jiangsu Province navigated China's multi-level PV governance framework from 1997 to 2024. The paper examines three competing theoretical frameworks — state-led mobilization, adaptive participatory governance, and hybrid governance — and evaluates which best explains Jiangsu's development trajectory. It argues for a profit-centered hybrid model in which state frameworks set directions and boundaries, but enterprise profit motives and market fluctuations drive actual behavior.

## Methods

Qualitative process tracing; systematic review of provincial policy documents from six state agencies (1997–2024); analysis of patent data, installed capacity, power generation, and utilization hour statistics.

## Data

- China National Intellectual Property Administration (CNIPA) Patent Search and Analysis Platform
- Electric Power Yearbooks of China, 2013–2021
- Policy documents from Jiangsu Development and Reform Commission, Industry and Information Technology Department of Jiangsu Province, Jiangsu Energy Regulatory Office (NEA), People's Government of Jiangsu Province, and Department of Ecology and Environment of Jiangsu Province

## Key finding

Before 2011, favorable national policy frameworks did not stimulate domestic PV diffusion; the real turning point was the collapse of export demand after anti-dumping rulings. Subsidies shaped the timing and scale of expansion but not its technological depth. Policy functioned reactively rather than proactively, following market excesses rather than anticipating them.
